Dwarka location_on

Dwarka, located in the western Indian state of Gujarat, is a significant pilgrimage site for Hindus. It is believed to be the ancient kingdom of Lord Krishna and holds immense religious and historical importance.

Dwarkadhish Temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, is the main attraction in Dwarka. It is believed to be over 2,500 years old and is an architectural marvel. The temple's spire rises to a height of 78 meters and dominates the skyline of Dwarka.

Rukmini Devi Temple Situated around 2 kilometers away from the Dwarkadhish Temple, this temple is dedicated to Rukmini, the chief queen of Lord Krishna. It is said that the temple was constructed overnight by Lord Krishna's grandson, Aniruddha.

Nageshwar Jyotirlinga Temple located around 17 kilometers from Dwarka, is one of the 12 Jyotirlinga shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is believed to be one of the holiest Shiva temples in India.

Dwarka offers a variety of shopping opportunities for tourists, including traditional handicrafts, textiles, jewelry, and souvenirs. The local markets around the Dwarkadhish Temple are particularly popular among visitors.

Overall, Dwarka attracts thousands of devotees and tourists every year due to its rich religious heritage, historical significance, and serene natural beauty.
